Annotating a Nanoparticle
Only users with PI permissions can annotate a nanoparticle. For more information, see Permissions and Visibility. You can access this page after logging into caNanoLab or you can access publicly available nanoparticle information from the Login page.
Annotating a nanoparticle involves selecting the nanoparticle to annotate, entering general information about the nanoparticle, submitting the particle function, and entering the particle characterizations (physical, in vitro) via the Navigation Tree. Note that annotating a nanoparticle with characterizations includes submitting the source of the characterization, the instrument used to perform the characterization, where appropriate, an appropriate protocol, the characterization information itself, and relevant information in the form of uploaded files. A single particle can have multiple annotations, either coming from different sources, or the same data source being tested under different conditions (e.g. temperature, solvents, instruments).
